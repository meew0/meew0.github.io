Discord Bots survey results

These are the results from the survey we held recently, plus some selected results from the old (March 2017) survey whose results were never published before. The results are just for informational purposes right now; we will be making changes over time based on them. If you want to make a specific point based on something you read here (or if you want to suggest some other chart/correlation to be included), DM me at meew0#9811

There were 99 responses in total to the new survey, and 227 to the old one. The total number of people who answered a particular question is specified with each question. "Other" answers are not always included in pie charts, so the sum of answers in a pie chart may not correspond 100% to the number of responses.

Also, a general note about the questions: most of the questions from the new survey, especially the demographic ones, were initially written by Roadcrosser, with the other mods making improvements later on. The survey was only sent out after all mods gave their approval. The old survey questions were basically only written by myself.

General questions about the server

About #information vs. #bot-list (93 responses)

A divisive issue: a slight majority of people are fine with the current setup. Those who want the channels to be rewritten seem to prefer them being merged, though. Other notable opinions:

  • A few people wanted #bot-list to be merged into #newspaper, which I guess makes sense as #bot-list is also an announcement channel nowadays.
  • Some people specifically critiqued the information in the channels as being outdated (e.g. on selfbots).

Lack of explicitly defined rules (38 responses)

This question was specifically not a question where answers could be selected, because I wanted the people who felt strongly about this issue to provide reasoning why they think so. A surprising (to me) amount of people answered this question. A quick estimate shows that 22 answers were generally positive of the current system, 12 answers were generally negative, and 4 answers were neutral or off-topic. Several of the negative answers didn't provide much reasoning beyond "other servers do it this way, so we should too", which is what I feared would happen. Although, many positive answers didn't provide a reasoning either. Arguments given against the current system include:

  • Giving the moderators freedom may lead to abuse and arbitrariness (although several people say this is not as much of an issue on DBots as it is on other servers they know)
  • People cannot always be trusted to behave according to common sense, or may use the lack of rules as an excuse for anarchy
  • It is annoying when people ask about specific situations, or those situations have to be explained to them
Arguments given for the current system include:
  • Giving the moderators freedom leads to tolerance for situations that would be considered OK but would be forbidden by the rules. (One example given here was people talking in foreign languages as a joke, for a short time)
  • Rules cannot cover every situation, and people will find loopholes in the rules or use the lack of specific rules as an excuse to do those things, even if they would not be considered OK
  • People can usually be trusted to behave according to common sense
  • People who plan to misbehave wouldn't read the rules anyway
Overall, while there were negative responses, it seems like there isn't a huge push to change the status quo. Another suggested solution was having explicit rules like "Use common sense" and "Moderators' say is final".

About #newspaper (93 responses)

I was initially opposed to #newspaper, but enough people think that a channel for not-as-important announcements is warranted that we will most likely keep it. Other notable opinions:

  • Some people suggested renaming it to #announcements to reflect its purpose more clearly. I think this is not a good idea as then people may think there is no other channel for more important announcements, and will only pay attention to #newspaper
  • Some people took issue with unprofessionalism in #newspaper (e.g. road's presidential alerts), and/or wanted a clear format for announcements.
  • One person suggested pinging people more for announcements

About the testing channels (92 responses)

Pretty clear situation, most people think the current number of testing channels is fine. I see no need for change here.

About the voice channels (94 responses)

This situation is not as clear as the testing channels. Many people think there are too many testing voice channels. Several people also specifically noted that they don't care about the voice channels, which is probably understandable. Other concerns noted:

  • Several people specifically wanted more music voice channels, including ones that are limited to certain "verified music bots".
  • Several people also noted that there is not much of a difference in usage between testing and music VCs.
  • One person wanted voice activity to be enabled in the general VC; others also wanted more general voice channels for concurrent conversations.
  • Some people proposed a mechanism where testing voice channels are automatically cleared out after some time.
In general, there is no clear course of action here. The current situation doesn't appear to be terrible, but there could still be changes.

About #find-a-bot and #development (93 responses)

Nothing to be done here, really. Most of the "other" answers consisted of people noting their indifference; several people also praised #find-a-bot specifically for being very useful. #development was occasionally noted as being a bit slow to get responses from.

New #general without bots (91 responses)

This is a divisive issue again. The reason we have been historically reluctant to make this move is that the server revolves around discussion of bots, and there would be little point in such a discussion if there is no way to quickly refer to a specfiic bot by using it. Only recently was #find-a-bot changed to not allow most bots. I don't see a very clear course of action here either. Most of the "other" section consisted of indifference again.

Mod coverage in your time zone (90 responses)

Note: I collected all "Other" answers that noted differing coverage based on time of day or other factors into a "So/so" category.

All people who answered "Bad" and specified their time zone later on (11 people) were in the eastern hemisphere (positive time zones). Most commonly this was UTC+3 (four people) and adjacent time zones (six people in total). Three people also answered "Bad" in the +10/+11 time zones. One person in UTC+5:30 and one person in UTC+7 also answered "Bad".

Other suggestions (32 responses)

Many people suggested things here which they already noted in an "Other" field above. Apart from those, notes people made include:

  • Several people criticised the site version v2 not being available yet.
  • One person wanted instructions for how to check whether a bot is in the queue to be provided.
  • Several people complained about the slow verification time.
  • One person actually wanted more memeing in #newspaper, unlike some of the respondents above.
  • One person suggested a system where misbehaving bots that don't create emergencies are "flagged" and will appear in an internal channel, and mods will occasionally look into that channel and deal with those bots.
  • Several people suggested different systems for bot devs being made more aware for their work, like having a channel where specific bots are featured, and some sort of certification program.
  • One person wanted to get rid of #ultimate-shitposting, as there are too many bots for it to be useful.
Some people also suggested more or less harsh moderation in reference to specific situations; we'll look into those. (If you have a specific complaint, feel free to DM me)

Comments

The comments for each mod pretty much reflect their ratings, and there were no particularly interesting or funny ones, so I'm skipping a detailed analysis of the comments for each mod here. Some users noted specific situations in which a mod did things wrong; if you sent one of those in and want the situation to be looked into more than just the mod being made aware of the comment, please DM me (meew0#9811) with detailed information, proof, etc.

Final comments

There were a great variety of comments sent in here. Some highlights:

  • One person said it was bad to rate the moderators. While I was also concerned about this initially, it was actually one of the mods who suggested it this time, and the others were fine with it. The mods are definitely professional enough to handle bad ratings! While I agree that in an ideal world the mods would just be equal to normal users except with permissions, in practicality it's useful to do things like this in order to improve the situation even more.
  • Thanks to the people who took their time to write out paragraphs of critique; I genuinely appreciate this!
  • Many people commented on v2 not being available yet, or the website needing an overhaul in general.
  • Some people complained about specific moderation decisions and other specific problems; if you want them to be looked into further, DM me (same as above)
  • A few people complained about the style of the server, in that it is too weeaboo-y, too NSFW, too cutesy, etc. If you think something specific is NSFW please ping the mods about it when it happens; if the other things annoy you to a point where you can't be active on the server, then it probably isn't for you...
  • Many other people said the community is great! So if you are one of the people contributing to the above (except NSFW) don't feel bad about it
  • To the person who sent in "person reading this cute": thanks :) I guess that goes equally for the person reading this!

Age on the server (96 responses)

Not entirely unsurprisingly, most people who answered the survey were already here for a while. What's interesting is that "Several days" is a larger category than "Several weeks", suggesting that people sometimes leave again after being here for some days.

Age (95 responses)

The 13-24 age bracket clearly dominates the server. Out of "other", two people were between 25 and 34 years old, one between 35 and 44, and one 45 years or older. One person claimed to be 12 or younger, which would make it illegal for them to be on Discord (this was noted in the question). I would honestly have expected more people to choose that as a meme, but I'm glad people were apparently mostly serious here :)
